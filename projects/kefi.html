Project

Kefi Data Science System

Python, SQL, PostgreSQL, Pandas, Prophet, Experimentation, KPI dashboards
Overview

I started Kefi during undergraduate studies in the COVID-19 period to build a sustainable fashion brand and generate revenue. The brand was featured as a student entrepreneur in The Indian Express on April 11, 2021, reflecting early recognition and traction in the fashion space.

Business problem

As the business grew across products and seasons, manual tracking made it difficult to answer core questions quickly, which products drive revenue, who returns to purchase, when demand spikes, and how pricing or presentation changes affect conversion. The goal was to convert operations data into a reliable analytics system that supports inventory, pricing, and growth decisions.

What I built
  • Built end-to-end ETL and analytics pipeline (Python, SQL, PostgreSQL, Pandas) processing 25K+ transactions and 10K+ customer records.
  • Queried multi-table datasets using JOINs, GROUP BY, window functions, and aggregations to analyze customer retention, repeat purchase behavior, and product performance.
  • Designed data validation and cleaning checks improving data consistency and reducing reporting errors.
  • Conducted A/B experiments (pricing and product presentation) across 1K+ user sessions, applied two-proportion Z-tests, t-tests, p-values, and 95% confidence intervals to measure significance and optimize revenue.
  • Built time-series forecasting model (Prophet) predicting seasonal demand to support inventory planning during peak periods.
  • Developed KPI dashboard tracking Revenue, AOV, Refund %, Retention, and Conversion Rate for decision-ready monitoring.
Results
  • Created a single source of truth for customer, order, and product analytics, reducing reliance on manual reporting.
  • Enabled retention and repeat-purchase analysis to focus marketing and inventory on higher-performing segments and SKUs.
  • Reduced reporting errors through validation and cleaning checks.
  • Improved decision-making on pricing and presentation through statistically tested experiments.
  • Lowered stockout risk during peak periods by forecasting seasonal demand and planning inventory accordingly.
