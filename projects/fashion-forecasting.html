Project

Fashion Trends Forecasting

Python, Google Trends, Prophet, TFT, Rolling-origin backtests
Overview

A time-series forecasting project that turns trend signals into a reproducible dataset and produces 12-week forecasts for planning.

Business problem

Trend-driven demand is hard to plan for because it shifts with seasonality and events. The goal was to forecast near-term trends and quantify uncertainty, so decisions can be based on backtested model performance rather than intuition.

What I built
  • Built reproducible dataset from Google Trends (5-year weekly) and engineered seasonal and holiday features.
  • Trained Prophet and TFT with rolling-origin backtests and compared against a naïve seasonal baseline using MAPE, sMAPE, and MASE.
  • Produced 12-week forecasts with confidence intervals and deployed a Streamlit dashboard for exploration.
Results
  • Delivered a repeatable forecasting workflow with rolling-origin evaluation for honest performance measurement.
  • Provided 12-week forecasts with confidence intervals to support planning and seasonal strategy.
  • Enabled rapid scenario review through an interactive Streamlit dashboard.
